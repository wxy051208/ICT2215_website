The Welcoming Pine

Rime

Xidi and Hongcun

The Welcoming Pine, scientifically known as Huangshan Pine, is located in Huangshan Yiping Scenic Area, Huangshan City, Anhui Province. It stands at an altitude of 1,680 meters and grows on a rock beside a stone lion. The tree is 10.08 meters tall and has a trunk circumference of 2.24 meters. It is approximately 1,000 years old. The Welcoming Pine, like a hospitable host, stretches out its arms to warmly welcome guests from all directions. With its vigorous and upright posture, it ranks first among the "Top Ten Famous Pines of Huangshan" and is one of the "Five Wonders" of Huangshan, enjoying the titles of "National Treasure" and "The World's Most Beautiful Pine". It is also a symbol of Anhui Province.In 1959, on the eve of the founding of the People's Republic of China, the Beijing Great Hall of the People was completed. The Anhui Provincial Committee selected a large iron painting titled "The Welcoming Pine" and displayed it in the Anhui Hall. Later, Premier Zhou Enlai personally decided to move it to the Reception Hall of the Great Hall of the People as a symbol of the warmth and hospitality of the Chinese people, and as a symbol of the peaceful friendship between the Chinese people and the people of the world.

Rime is a common natural phenomenon in winter. When the temperature at the mountain top is very low and there is sufficient moisture in the air, the water in the fog will directly condense onto the branches and leaves of pine trees and the surface of rocks, forming a layer of white and crystal-clear ice.From a distance, it looks like the trees are covered with white flowers; up close, it is a row of tiny ice crystals attached to the branches, which will shine brightly in the sunlight.Because it requires both low temperature and cloud fog to occur simultaneously, it is not a phenomenon that can be seen every day. It is most likely to appear in the early morning of deep winter, and precisely for this reason, it is particularly precious and romantic.

Huizhou culture is one of the most distinctive regional cultures in China, known for its rich history, traditional values, and unique artistic style.The culture is reflected in architecture, local customs, and traditional crafts. White walls, black tiles, and elegant wooden carvings are key features of Huizhou-style buildings.This cultural heritage gives the Huangshan region not only natural beauty, but also deep historical significance.

Xidi and Hongcun are both representative examples of the Huizhou cultural architecture.

Hongcun is famous for its unique layout and peaceful water system. The village looks like a traditional Chinese painting, with reflections of houses in the water.Xidi is known for its well-preserved ancient architecture and historical atmosphere. Walking through the village feels like stepping back in time.
