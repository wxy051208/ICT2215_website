About Huangshan

Huangshan is a national scenic spot, a 5A-level tourist attraction, a national civilized scenic and tourist area, and the only mountain among China's top ten scenic spots. It is also a nature reserve that integrates World Cultural and Natural Heritage, a World Geopark, and a World Biosphere Reserve. It is renowned worldwide for its five wonders: unique pines, strange rocks, sea of clouds, hot springs, and winter snow. Together with the Yangtze River, the Great Wall, and the Yellow River, it is an important symbol of the magnificent landscapes of the Chinese nation and is hailed by the world as a "paradise on earth" and "the most extraordinary mountain under heaven". Within its territory, numerous peaks vie in beauty and strange rocks stand in profusion. There are 88 peaks over 1,000 meters high, with the three main peaks of Lotus Peak, Bright Summit, and Heavenly Capital Peak all exceeding 1,800 meters in altitude.

Geographic Position

Huangshan Mountain stands majestically in the southern part of Anhui Province, within the Huangshan City. The mountain area is approximately 40 kilometers long from north to south and 30 kilometers wide from east to west, with a total area of about 1,200 square kilometers. Among them, the Huangshan Scenic Area covers an area of 160.6 square kilometers, spanning from east longitude 118°01' to 118°17' and from north latitude 30°01' to 30°18'. It starts from Huangshi Cave in the east, reaches Xiaoliangjiao in the west, begins at Erlong Bridge in the east, and ends at Tangkou Town in the south. It is divided into nine management zones: hot springs, Yunluo, Yuping, Beihai, Songgu, Daoqiao, Fuji, Yanghu, and FuGu. The buffer zone covers an area of 490.9 square kilometers, with the administrative boundary adjacent to the scenic area being the five towns and one forest farm (Tangkou Town, Tanjiaqiao Town, Sankou Town, Gengcheng Town, Jiaonan Town and Yanghu Forest Farm) in the east.
