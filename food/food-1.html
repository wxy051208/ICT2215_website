Huangshan Stinky Mandarin Fish is one of the most iconic and representative dishes of the Huizhou region. The fish is prepared through a carefully controlled fermentation process, which produces its signature strong aroma. Although the smell may seem intense at first, the flavor is surprisingly delicate, tender, and deeply savory. The cooking process enhances the natural taste of the fish while preserving its soft texture. Widely regarded as a must-try local specialty, this dish perfectly illustrates the distinctive flavor profile and culinary craftsmanship of Huizhou Cuisine

Mao Tofu is a distinctive traditional delicacy of Huizhou Cuisine, renowned for its unique fermentation process. During this process, a layer of fine white “hair” naturally develops on the surface of the tofu, giving it both its name and characteristic appearance. Despite its unusual look, Mao Tofu is prized for its rich, savory flavor and soft, creamy texture. It is typically pan-fried or braised, creating a pleasant contrast between a lightly crispy exterior and a tender interior. This dish reflects the ingenuity of local culinary techniques and the Huizhou people’s appreciation for bold and complex flavors

Huangshan Maofeng Tea is a highly regarded Chinese green tea produced in the scenic Yellow Mountain area. It is celebrated for its fresh aroma, clear pale-green infusion, and smooth, refreshing taste with a subtle sweetness. The tea leaves are slender, slightly curled, and often covered with fine white hairs, indicating their premium quality. Carefully hand-picked and processed using traditional techniques, Huangshan Maofeng embodies both natural purity and refined craftsmanship. Beyond its flavor, it also represents the harmony between nature and culture that defines the Huizhou region.
